# Sara Environment Bible

## Purpose

This document defines how locations are represented in Sara's content.

Environment consistency matters as much as character consistency.

A viewer should be able to recognize the visual world of this project.

---

## Core Principle

**Real locations, represented honestly.**

Do not fabricate generic "Middle Eastern" or "Arabian" environments.

Every location Sara visits should feel like the specific place it is.

---

## Reference Images First

Reference images are always preferred over long environment descriptions.

When producing content for a new location:
1. Collect reference images first — see `/references` folder
2. Use images as the primary source for visual direction
3. Add descriptive notes only where images are insufficient

A good reference image is worth more than a paragraph of description.

---

## Location Documentation Standard

Every location used in production should have a reference entry.

### Reference Entry Format

```
Location: [Name]
City/Region: [City, Country]
Type: [Landmark / Neighborhood / Natural / Cultural]
Description: [2-3 sentences maximum]
Key Visual Elements: [List 3-5 specific visual details]
Reference Images: [Filenames or folder path]
Notes: [Any important accuracy or cultural notes]
```

## Jeddah (Home Base)

Jeddah is Sara's home city and the default starting point for the project.

### Al-Balad (Historic District)

* UNESCO World Heritage Site
* Coral-stone buildings with wooden Rawasheen (latticed bay windows), typically 5–7 stories tall with a narrow profile — taller than most traditional Middle Eastern architecture
* Narrow alleys that feel canyon-like due to the height-to-width ratio of the buildings
* Faded colors: ochre, sand, dusty white, traces of blue and green on doors and window frames
* Old merchant houses — some restored, some in original condition, some awaiting restoration
* The smell of incense and old wood is part of the atmosphere
* Reference folder: `/references/jeddah/al-balad/` — create this subfolder before producing Al-Balad content

#### Al-Balad: Living Neighborhood Rule

Al-Balad is not a museum. It is not a ruin. It is a living, inhabited neighborhood.

People live in the historic buildings. There are families, elderly residents, small merchants, craftsmen, children. Street vendors operate in certain lanes. Cats are everywhere. Motorcycles are parked in alleys. Laundry hangs from some windows. Satellite dishes appear on rooftops alongside the Rawasheen.

This everyday life coexisting with architecture that is over a thousand years old is the most important story about Al-Balad.

**Production rule:** All Al-Balad environments must include at least one sign of active habitation. An empty alley with no signs of life looks like a film set, not a real neighborhood.

**Avoid:**
* Crumbling ruins aesthetic
* Completely empty environments
* Abandoned or derelict framing
* Any visual language that suggests Al-Balad is dying or deserted

### Jeddah Corniche

* Long coastal promenade along the Red Sea
* Wide open sky, sea breeze, palm trees
* King Fahd Fountain visible from the northern Corniche — not from all points; only reference it when the scene location is correct
* People walking, families, movement — the Corniche is active at most hours
* Red Sea color near Jeddah: clear turquoise in shallow water shifting to deep blue offshore — not generic blue-green
* Light is best at golden hour AM and PM
* Reference folder: `/references/jeddah/corniche/` — create this subfolder before producing Corniche content

### Al-Shallal Area / North Jeddah

* Modern face of the city
* Wide roads, malls, sea views
* Contrast with Al-Balad
* Reference folder: `/references/jeddah/north/` — create this subfolder before producing North Jeddah content

## Environment Categories

### Urban Historical
Old city cores, traditional architecture, narrow streets.

Visual priorities:
* Texture of walls and surfaces
* Natural light and shadow
* Human scale — show how small people are relative to old structures
* Age and weathering are part of the aesthetic, not defects

### Coastal
Seafronts, harbors, beaches, waterways.

Visual priorities:
* Horizon line relationship to the subject
* Water color and movement
* Time of day is critical — avoid midday harsh light

### Desert and Natural
Open landscapes, dunes, mountains, wadi valleys.

Visual priorities:
* Scale — vast and empty is part of the point
* Light quality — desert light is warm and directional
* Silence is part of the environment description

### International Urban
Cities outside Saudi Arabia — Istanbul, Cairo, Marrakech, etc.

Visual priorities:
* Identify the specific neighborhood, not just the city
* Use recognizable landmarks as anchors
* Do not mix architectural styles from different cities

## Lighting Standards

Preferred lighting for Sara in all environments:

| Time of Day      | Use                                      |
|------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Golden hour AM   | Primary — warm, directional, beautiful   |
| Golden hour PM   | Primary — storytelling, moody            |
| Overcast day     | Acceptable — soft, even light            |
| Midday harsh     | Avoid unless narratively required        |
| Night / artificial| Use sparingly — must feel intentional   |

---

## What to Avoid

* Generic "Arabic city" environments with no specific identity.
* Overly saturated or fantasy-looking environments.
* Environments that feel like stock footage or stock renders.
* Inconsistent architecture that mixes cultural styles without reason.
* Empty environments that feel lifeless — some human presence adds realism.
