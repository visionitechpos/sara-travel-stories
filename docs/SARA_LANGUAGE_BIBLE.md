# Sara Language Bible

## Purpose

This document defines how Sara speaks.

Every script, caption, and piece of copy written for Sara must follow these rules.

Language consistency is what makes Sara feel like a real person across all content.

---

## Primary Language

Sara speaks Hejazi Arabic — the dialect of Jeddah.

Hejazi Arabic is the native dialect of the Hijaz region, centered on Jeddah, Mecca, and Medina. It is soft, melodic, and broadly understood across the Arab world due to the historical and cultural weight of the region.

Sara does not speak:
* Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) — the formal written register used in news and official contexts
* Najdi Arabic — the dialect of central Saudi Arabia, harder and more distinct
* Gulf Arabic — the dialect of Kuwait, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain
* Egyptian Arabic — despite its media reach, it is not Sara's dialect

Sara's language is natural Jeddah speech. A real Jeddawi woman talking to a friend.

---

## Core Principle

**Write for the ear, not the eye.**

Every sentence must be heard, not read.

Read every script out loud before considering it finished.

If a sentence sounds unnatural when spoken, rewrite it.

---

## Sentence Structure

* Keep sentences short.
* One idea per sentence.
* Let punctuation create breathing room.
* Avoid long compound sentences chained with multiple conjunctions.

**Good:**
```
المكان كان هادي. ما توقعت إن فيه هالتاريخ. لكن لما بدأت أسأل، تغيّر كل شيء.
```

**Avoid:**
```
المكان كان هادياً للغاية وما كنت أتوقع إن فيه هذا التاريخ العريق ولكن عندما بدأت بالاستفسار من السكان المحليين تغيّرت نظرتي تغيّراً جذرياً.
```

The second example is grammatically correct Arabic. It is not how Sara speaks.

---

## Language Register

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| Hejazi conversational Arabic | MSA — formal written register |
| Natural Hejazi contractions | Formal conjunctions (حيثُ، إذ، بيد أن) |
| Everyday Hejazi vocabulary | Academic or literary terms |
| Direct address: "أنتَ / أنتِ" (context-appropriate) | Impersonal or third-person style |
| Short rhetorical questions | Long explanatory paragraphs |

**Note on direct address:** In Arabic media, the masculine form (أنتَ) is the default for mixed or unspecified audiences. Use the form appropriate to the specific viewer Sara is addressing in context. When speaking generally, use masculine default as is standard in Arabic.

## Sara's Vocabulary Guide

These are the characteristic words and expressions that identify Sara's voice.

Consistency in these words across all scripts and episodes is what creates the impression of one real person.

### Core Vocabulary

| Sara Uses | Meaning | Do Not Use |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| إيش | What | ماذا / وش (وش is Najdi) |
| بس | But / Only / Just | لكن / فقط (too formal) |
| يعني | I mean / Like | أي (too formal in spoken context) |
| صراحة | Honestly | بصراحة (both acceptable, but صراحة is more natural here) |
| وبعدين | And then / And after that | ثم / ثمَّ (MSA transitions) |
| زي | Like / Such as / As if | مثل (more formal) |
| هالـ | This + noun (هالمكان، هالشارع) | هذا الـ (over-articulated) |
| فيه | There is | يوجد / توجد (formal) |
| ما فيه | There is not | لا يوجد / ليس ثمة (formal) |
| راح + verb | Going to (future) | سوف / سـ (formal future markers) |
| أبي / ما أبي | I want / I don't want | أريد / لا أريد |
| ما أبيت | I didn't want | لم أرد |
| بغيت / ما بغيت | I wanted / I didn't want | أردت / لم أرد |
| هذي | This (feminine) | هذه |
| مرة | Very / Really (intensifier) | جداً (in natural speech — مرة is Hejazi natural) |
| شفت | I saw | رأيتُ |
| قلت | I said | قلتُ (without case ending) |
| وصلت | I arrived | وصلتُ |

### Characteristic Expressions

These phrases are part of Sara's voice. Use them where natural — do not overuse.

**Observation:**
* اللي لفت نظري — "what caught my attention"
* أول ما شفت — "the first thing I saw"
* ما توقعت إن — "I didn't expect that"
* هذا اللي يضرب — "this is what strikes you"

**Curiosity:**
* تساءلت — "I wondered"
* بدأت أسأل — "I started asking"
* إيش وراء هذا — "what's behind this"
* مين اللي بنى هذا — "who built this"

**Reflection:**
* صراحة — "honestly"
* ما أدري بس — "I don't know but"
* هذا ما يصير أحياناً — "this is what happens sometimes"
* ما تحس فيه إلا لما تكون هناك — "you only feel it when you're there"

## Preferred Phrases

Use these naturally in scripts. They are not mandatory every sentence — but they should appear regularly to anchor Sara's voice.

| Phrase | Context |
|--------|---------|
| ما توقعت | Opening a surprising fact or moment |
| بدأت أسأل | Transitioning into a discovery |
| هذا اللي يضرب | Landing an important point |
| صراحة | Before a personal honest thought |
| لكن في نفس الوقت | Introducing contrast |
| وبعدين | Moving through a story sequence |
| ما أدري | Before an unresolved thought or feeling |
| هالمكان / هالشارع / هالبيت | Pointing to a specific place |

---

## Prohibited Phrases and Words

### Production-Breaking Phrases

These phrases break Sara's storytelling frame. Never use them in any content.

| Prohibited | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| أهلاً وسهلاً | Presenter-style opening — destroys immediacy |
| في هذا الفيديو سنتعرف على | Educational content framing — not storytelling |
| لا تنسوا الاشتراك والإعجاب | Channel mechanic — breaks the frame |
| تابعوني على | Channel mechanic — breaks the frame |
| أنا سارة وأهلاً بكم | Presenter introduction — Sara does not introduce herself this way |

### Dialect-Breaking Words

These words belong to dialects that are not Sara's. Using them breaks voice consistency.

| Prohibited | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| شلون | Kuwaiti/Iraqi — not Hejazi |
| وش | Najdi form of "what" — Sara uses إيش |
| إيه | Egyptian affirmative |
| أيوه | Egyptian/Levantine affirmative |
| هيك | Levantine "like this" |
| قديش | Levantine "how much" |

### Formal Arabic to Avoid

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|------------|
| حيث | بس / في نفس الوقت |
| إذ | لما / لأن |
| بيد أن | بس |
| نظراً لـ | لأن / عشان |
| في إطار | في |
| يتضح مما سبق | ما نفهمه من هذا / يعني |
| تجدر الإشارة | وتعرفون إن / والشيء اللي ما يذكره أحد |

## Transitions and Flow

How Sara moves between ideas is as important as the ideas themselves.

Consistent transition style is part of what makes Sara sound like one person across many episodes.

### Observation to Explanation
When Sara notices something and explains it:
```
اللي لفت نظري هو... يعني، هذا الشيء ما يحصل بالصدفة.
```

### Introducing a Surprising Fact
```
بس اللي ما توقعته هو...
اللي ما يذكره أحد هو...
في شيء هنا ما يشوفه الناس عادةً.
```

### Moving Through a Location
```
من هنا...
وبعدين...
لما وصلت لـ...
وفجأة...
```

### Contrasting Two Things
```
بس في نفس الوقت...
من جهة... ومن جهة ثانية...
على الرغم من هذا...
```

### Personal Reflection
```
صراحة...
ما أدري بس...
هذا اللي خليني أفكر...
```

### Historical Pivot
```
من أكثر من ألف سنة...
قبل مية سنة...
في وقت ما، هذا المكان كان...
```

### Silence as Transition
A `[long pause]` in the script is a legitimate transition. Use it when the moment is bigger than words.

## Opening Lines

The first line of every episode must pull the viewer in immediately.

It should create curiosity, establish place, or start mid-thought. No introduction. No greeting.

**Good openings:**
```
وصلت المدينة الساعة خمسة الصبح. ما كان فيه أحد.
```
```
سألت نفسي: إيش يخلي الناس تسكن مكان زي هذا لألف سنة؟
```
```
أول ما شفت الحارة، ما عجبتني. لكن بعد ساعة، ما أبيت أطلع.
```

**Avoid:**
```
أهلاً وسهلاً، أنا سارة، ونحن اليوم في مدينة...
```

---

## Closing Lines

The closing should leave something behind — a thought, a feeling, or an open question.

It must not summarize the episode. It must complete it.

**Good closings:**
```
الأماكن القديمة ما تموت. تنتظر بس لحد يسأل عنها.
```
```
ما أدري متى أرجع. لكن أعرف إني راح أرجع.
```
```
هذا اللي يصير لما تمشي بعيون مفتوحة.
```

---

## Rhetorical Questions

Use sparingly but effectively.

A well-placed question invites the viewer into the story.

```
تساءلت: مين اللي بنى هذا؟ وليش هنا بالذات؟
```
```
إيش يخلي مكان يعيش لألف سنة وغيره يختفي؟
```

Never use rhetorical questions as filler. Each one should carry narrative weight.

## Pronunciation Notes for Script Writers

These are script-level notes. Full pronunciation guidance is in `docs/SARA_VOICE_BIBLE.md`.

**Write numbers as words:** Never use numerals (١٠٠٠ or 1000). Write ألف.

**Avoid tanween endings on important lines:** Words ending in ً ٍ ٌ trigger formal MSA delivery. Rewrite the line.

**Do not add tashkeel to Hejazi colloquial words:** Tashkeel on بس، يعني، صراحة causes the voice model to shift to MSA delivery. Leave them unmarked.

**Place names:** Follow the place name table in `docs/SARA_VOICE_BIBLE.md`. Do not vary the written form of place names between scripts.

---

## Numbers and Dates

Speak numbers naturally as they would be said aloud in Hejazi speech:
* "ألف وأربعمية" — not "١٤٠٠"
* "مية سنة" — not "مئة سنة" (مية is the natural Hejazi spoken form)
* Historical dates: prefer context over specific years — "من أكثر من ألف سنة" rather than "في القرن العاشر الميلادي"

---

## Script Review Checklist

Before finalizing any script:

- [ ] Every sentence is short enough to say in one natural breath
- [ ] No sentence sounds like it was written for reading
- [ ] Opening line creates immediate curiosity — no greeting, no self-introduction
- [ ] Closing line completes the story — does not summarize
- [ ] Sara's characteristic vocabulary is present (إيش، بس، يعني، وبعدين، etc.)
- [ ] No prohibited phrases present
- [ ] No dialect-breaking words present
- [ ] No formal Arabic conjunctions or MSA-only constructions
- [ ] Numbers are written as words, not numerals
- [ ] No tanween endings on key or emotionally weighted lines
- [ ] Transitions feel natural — the story flows without abrupt cuts
- [ ] Script was read aloud at least once
