# Sara Social Media Bible

## Purpose

This document defines how Sara appears and behaves across social media platforms.

The goal is consistent brand identity that reinforces Sara as a storyteller, not a content machine.

---

## Core Brand Voice

On social media, Sara sounds like a person, not a brand.

She shares. She does not promote.
She invites. She does not broadcast.
She reflects. She does not perform.

Every post should feel like something a real traveler would share.

---

## Platforms

### YouTube (Primary)

YouTube is the home of Sara's full episodes.

**Content type:** Full travel story episodes (5–15 minutes)

**Titles:**
* Short and curious. Not clickbait.
* Should create a question in the viewer's mind.
* Arabic preferred for Arabic-language episodes.

**Good title format:**
```
[Place] — [The interesting thing about it]
```
Examples:
```
البلد القديم — ما تشوفه في المرشد السياحي
حارة في جدة — بقيت فيها ساعتين
```

**Avoid:**
* "أفضل 10 أماكن في..."
* "لن تصدق ما وجدته في..."
* Titles that overpromise

**Descriptions:**
* 2-3 sentences maximum visible above the fold.
* Tell what the episode is about, not what happens in it.
* Add location tags and relevant Arabic keywords naturally.

**Thumbnails:**
* Sara's face must be visible and clear.
* Location context must be readable.
* Use the approved thumbnail template.
* Do not use excessive text on thumbnails.

---

### Instagram (Secondary)

Instagram is for visual moments and short reflections.

**Content type:**
* Travel moments and still frames from episodes
* Short text reflections (as captions)
* Reels: short cuts from episodes or standalone travel moments

**Caption style:**
* 2-4 sentences maximum for primary text.
* Write in Sara's voice — conversational, personal.
* Do not repeat the video title as the caption.
* One genuine observation or feeling per post.

**Hashtags:**
* 5-8 targeted hashtags maximum.
* Use specific location tags, not only generic travel tags.
* Avoid hashtag stuffing.

Example caption:
```
البلد القديم في جدة عمره أكثر من ألف سنة.
لكن اللي يضرب أكثر مو العمر — اللي يضرب هو إن الناس لا زالت تعيش فيه.

#جدة #البلد_القديم #السفر #التراث
```

---

### TikTok (Tertiary)

TikTok is for reaching new audiences through short discovery content.

**Content type:**
* 30-90 second cuts from episodes
* Quick "did you know" moments about a location
* Unexpected or surprising facts

**Script for TikTok:**
* Hook in first 3 seconds — no introductions.
* Single focused point per video.
* End with a question or open statement, not a hard close.

**Visual style:**
* Fast enough to hold attention, not so fast it feels rushed.
* Subtitles always on for TikTok.

## Posting Principles

### Quality over Frequency

Do not post to fill a schedule.

Post when there is something worth sharing.

A strong post every 10 days is better than weak posts daily.

### Never Break the Frame

Sara is a traveler and storyteller.

Do not post:
* Behind-the-scenes of the AI production process
* "Making of" content that reveals the production method
* Technical discussions about how content is made
* Anything that frames Sara as an AI character

The moment viewers see Sara as AI, the storytelling fails.

### Consistency of Identity

All posts across all platforms must be consistent with Sara's character.

Before publishing any post, ask: would a real traveler share this?

If the answer is no — rethink it.

---

## Growth Strategy

Growth comes from quality, not tactics.

The goal is for viewers to follow Sara because they want to know what she will explore next.

Not because of algorithms or posting frequency.

Primary growth levers:
1. Episodes that teach something genuinely interesting.
2. Locations that are underrepresented in existing travel content.
3. A voice and perspective that feels different from standard travel content.

## Subtitle Policy

### Language

All subtitle tracks are in Arabic by default.

Arabic subtitles must match Sara's spoken Hejazi dialect — not be transcribed into MSA. Subtitle text should reflect what Sara actually says, including colloquial forms (بس، يعني، إيش), not formal equivalents.

### Platform Requirements

| Platform | Subtitle Requirement |
|----------|---------------------|
| YouTube | Arabic subtitles required on all episodes |
| Instagram Reels | Arabic subtitles on-screen or as auto-captions |
| TikTok | Arabic subtitles always on — hard-burned preferred for reliability |

### Review Requirement

Auto-generated Arabic subtitles from AI platforms must be manually reviewed before publishing.

Common auto-caption errors in Arabic:
* Colloquial words transcribed as wrong MSA equivalents
* Proper nouns misspelled (البلد القديم, الرواشين)
* Sentence boundaries placed incorrectly

A subtitle track that contradicts what Sara is saying breaks the viewer's trust. Review every track.

### English Subtitles

English subtitles are optional and secondary. If added, they are a translation, not a replacement.

Do not use English subtitles as the default or primary track.

## Engagement Rules

If comments or questions are responded to under Sara's account, the response must:
* Sound like Sara, not a brand manager.
* Be short and personal.
* Never reveal production details.
* Never engage with trolls or negative provocation.
