## Story Summary

Sara stops, for the first time, in front of a large fish sculpture on the Obhur waterfront that she has passed many times without questioning. She asks someone nearby about it — he doesn't know either. This leads her to a broader realization: most of Jeddah's hundreds of public sculptures are unknown to the people who pass them daily. The episode closes without a resolved answer — the value is in the noticing, not the fact.

---

## Story Arc

**Opening:** Sara in front of the fish sculpture, stopping for real this time. The question surfaces immediately: why a fish, why here?

**Middle:** She asks a bystander, who doesn't know either. She observes that the surrounding area has changed completely (the waterfront redevelopment) while the fish itself stayed the same — the one constant. This expands into a realization about Jeddah's hundreds of anonymous public sculptures.

**Closing:** Sara doesn't resolve who designed it or why. She leaves with something more important: an awareness that she has been walking past unasked questions her whole life. The episode ends open, not concluded.

---

## Why This Episode Fits Sara

This is Sara's pilot episode — the natural origin point of her identity as a storyteller. She is not introduced formally; she is encountered mid-curiosity. The story models exactly what `STORY_FRAMEWORK.md` demands: genuine curiosity, a real unresolved question, and a discovery that reframes the ordinary. It also tests, for the first time in real production, the newly adopted principle "The Story Comes First" (`STORY_FRAMEWORK.md`) — no strong historical fact was available, and the episode was built to stand without one.

## Visual Approach

- Establishing shot: Sara at the fish sculpture, Obhur waterfront, morning light
- Medium/close shot: the sculpture itself, a detail Sara notices
- Two-shot or reverse shots: Sara and the bystander she asks
- Wide shot: the waterfront extending around her, showing the redeveloped promenade
- Closing shot: Sara looking around at the wider waterfront, unresolved expression

Per `SARA_ENVIRONMENT_BIBLE.md` lighting standards: golden hour AM preferred; avoid harsh midday light.
