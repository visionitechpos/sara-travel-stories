# Pilot Episode Candidate — The Object on the Corniche

**Status:** Concept capture. Not in development.
**Captured:** 2026-06-23

---

## Core Concept

Sara is walking along the Jeddah Corniche — not as a content creator, not with a plan.

She passes a public object: a sculpture, monument, or structure she has walked past many times before without understanding. This time, something makes her stop.

A genuine question emerges: **"What is that?"**

She researches the answer out of personal curiosity — not for an audience.

During that process she discovers that many ordinary objects and places around her have stories most people never learn. That discovery becomes the natural reason she begins sharing.

---

## Intent

* Sara is not introduced. She is encountered.
* The audience finds her through her curiosity, not through a formal opening.
* The episode feels accidental — something she did not plan to make.
* The story itself is the origin of Sara's journey as a storyteller.

---

## Story Type (candidate)

Everyday Depth — an object in plain sight, passed without question, that carries a story most people never learn.

Secondary possibility: Origin — what the object is, why it exists, why it was placed here.

To be confirmed when development begins.

---

## What Is Not Decided

* The specific object, sculpture, or structure.
* The specific story the object carries.
* Location precision on the Corniche.
* Whether the story resolves fully or opens into a larger question.

None of these are decided here. They are discovered during research.

---

## Notes

This episode is the pilot. Its purpose is to establish Sara as a character through a story — not through a formal introduction. Character consistency is more important than production ambition in a first episode.

The episode's success test (from README.md): a viewer finishes it thinking about the object and what it means — not about Sara's production.
